Owing to the great importance of grasses, both in natural plant communities and in
cultivated areas, and also the importance of certain taxa as cultivation indicators, it
would be highly desirable to be able to identify grasses to species. However, the
paucity of distinguishing characters sets very narrow limits. The Gramineae pollen
type is easily recognized, but also very monotonous. The characters that have been
most frequently used are quantitative: diameter of grain, ofpore and ofanulus. Like
all such characters their value is limited, especially as size seems to change with time.
